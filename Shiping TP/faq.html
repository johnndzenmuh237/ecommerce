What is a freight forwarder?
+
A freight forwarder is a logistics intermediary that arranges the transportation of goods on behalf of exporters and importers. 📡 Tracking

How do I track my shipment?
+
Visit our tracking page and enter your tracking number (format: SC-YYYY-XXXXXX), Bill of Lading number, container number, or booking reference. You'll get a full status timeline with location updates. You can also sign up for automatic email alerts at each milestone.
How often is tracking information updated?
+
Tracking updates depend on the shipment type. Ocean freight is typically updated at each port event (departure, arrival, transshipment). Air freight updates every 2–4 hours. Road shipments update with GPS position every 30 minutes while in transit.
My tracking shows no updates for several days. Is this normal?
+
During the main ocean voyage (vessel at sea), updates may be less frequent. For air and road, a gap of more than 24 hours is unusual. If you're concerned, contact your account manager or our operations team at ops@swiftcargo.com and they'll investigate immediately.

📋 Customs & Duties

Do you handle customs clearance?
+
Yes. SwiftCargo has licensed customs brokers in 80+ countries. We handle HS code classification, import/export declarations, duty calculation, document submission, and liaising with customs authorities on your behalf. Customs brokerage can be added to any freight service.
Who is responsible for paying import duties and taxes?
+
This depends on the agreed Incoterms. Under DDP (Delivered Duty Paid), the seller/exporter covers all duties. Under EXW or FOB, the importer/buyer is responsible. We can advise you on the best Incoterm for your trade relationship and provide duty estimates upfront.
What is an HS code and why does it matter?
+
The Harmonized System (HS) code is a standardized international commodity classification system. It determines the applicable duty rates, trade restrictions, and documentation requirements for your goods. An incorrect HS code can result in delays, penalties, or overpayment of duties — our customs team ensures accurate classification every time.

✈️ Air Freight

What is the transit time for air freight?
+
Transit times vary by route and service level. Express services (e.g. FedEx/DHL-integrated) typically deliver within 1–3 business days. Standard airfreight is usually 3–7 business days door-to-door, including customs clearance.
What are chargeable weight and volumetric weight?
+
Airlines charge based on whichever is greater: actual gross weight or volumetric (dimensional) weight. Volumetric weight = (L × W × H in cm) ÷ 6,000. For example, a large but lightweight box might have a higher volumetric weight than its actual weight, and you'll be charged the higher of the two.
Can you ship lithium batteries by air?
+
Yes, but with restrictions. Lithium batteries are classified as dangerous goods under IATA regulations. They must be properly packaged, labeled, and declared. Our DG specialists will ensure full compliance with IATA/ICAO regulations and airline-specific requirements.

🌊 Ocean Freight

What is the difference between FCL and LCL?
+
FCL (Full Container Load) means you book an entire container exclusively for your cargo — ideal for large shipments (typically 12+ CBM). LCL (Less than Container Load) means your cargo shares a container with other shippers, making it cost-effective for smaller shipments. SwiftCargo offers both options on all major trade lanes.
What are typical ocean freight transit times?
+
Transit times vary significantly by trade lane. Common routes: Asia–Europe: 25–35 days. Asia–US West Coast: 14–20 days. Asia–US East Coast: 25–35 days. Europe–US: 8–14 days. These are vessel transit times only; add 2–5 days for documentation, port handling, and customs clearance at each end.
What are Demurrage and Detention charges?
+
Demurrage is charged when a container remains at the port terminal beyond the free time after vessel arrival. Detention is charged when a container is kept outside the terminal (at your warehouse) beyond the carrier's free time. SwiftCargo helps you manage these timelines proactively to avoid unnecessary charges.

+
Yes. We offer All-Risk and Named Perils cargo insurance policies underwritten by leading marine insurers. Coverage is available for all modes of transport and can be arranged per shipment or as an open cover policy for regular shippers. Premiums are typically 0.1%–0.5% of cargo value.
